# Customer Segmentation Analysis Using K-Means Clustering

## Project Overview

This project focuses on customer segmentation using machine learning techniques. The objective is to group customers based on income and spending behavior to help businesses develop targeted marketing strategies and improve customer engagement.

Completed as part of the Oasis Infobyte Data Analytics Internship.

---

## Objectives

* Perform data cleaning and preprocessing
* Analyze customer income and spending behavior
* Create customer segments using K-Means Clustering
* Visualize customer groups
* Generate business insights and recommendations

---

## Dataset Information

The dataset contains customer demographic and spending information including:

* Income
* Kidhome
* Teenhome
* Recency
* MntWines
* MntFruits
* MntMeatProducts
* MntFishProducts
* MntSweetProducts
* MntGoldProds

A new feature named Total_Spending was created by combining all spending categories.

## Customer Segments

### VIP Customers

* Highest income and spending levels
* Most valuable customer group

### Loyal Customers

* Consistent purchasing behavior
* Largest customer segment

### Regular Customers

* Moderate income and spending
* Growth potential through targeted marketing

### Budget Customers

* Lower income and spending
* Respond well to discounts and promotions

---

## Key Insights

* Four customer segments were identified.
* VIP customers contribute significantly to revenue.
* Loyal customers represent the largest customer base.
* Income strongly influences spending behavior.
* Customer segmentation supports targeted marketing strategies.

---

## Conclusion

Customer segmentation using K-Means Clustering successfully identified distinct customer groups. The findings can help businesses improve customer targeting, retention, and revenue generation through data-driven decision-making.
